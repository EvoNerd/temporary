Psychology of Learning Study

MEEW: Mathematical Ecology and Evolution using Web apps

Digital tools have the potential to facilitate learning by:

  • reducing cognitive load (Skulmowski & Xu, 2021),
  • providing animated external representations (Schwonke et al., 2008), and
  • encouraging open-ended, inquiry learning (Griffith et al., 2024)

But when learning is too open-ended, it can lead to poor outcomes (Richter et al., 2022).


Teachers know that math is difficult for students, but we do not know why. One reason may be that students in Science Technology Engineering and Mathematics (STEM) are averse to math (Uttl et al., 2013; Wachsmuth et al., 2017). Interactive visualizations of math models (e.g., R Shiny apps) might:

  • bypass distress by focusing attention on biological concepts,
  • build key modeling skills, and
  • bolster long-term learning motivation.

Research Questions

Overall question: Can interactive web apps make students better at applied mathematical modelling in ecology and evolution?

  1. Does allowing students to interact with the app (i.e., as compared to demonstration only) improve their understanding?
  2. Do apps decrease students’ cognitive load as compared to traditional pen-and-paper math modelling exercises?
  3. Can learning with an app generally improve students’ mathematical modelling intuition and knowledge?

Methods

Our population of interest is ≥2nd year biology bachelor students from universities with English-language instruction. The online learning task takes 30 minutes. Participants are randomly assigned to one of three treatments. For reproducibility we have three case studies with mathematically similar models in different subject areas: evolution, infectious disease, and ecology. Each case study uses dynamical systems with alternative stable states and bistability. This enables us to assess a common set of learning outcomes; students will understand,

  1. the consequences of multiple attractors for the final state of the system,
  2. how the final state of the system is impacted by different parameter combinations, and
  3. how it is impacted by the initial conditions.

By comparing participants’ responses to case study-specific scientific questions before and after the learning task, we can identify how interactive apps can support different students in becoming better mathematical modelers.
